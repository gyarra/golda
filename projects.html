VALI MYERS
● ● ● A Memoir ● ● ● By Gianni Menichetti

“You saw in her the personalization of something torn and loose and deep down primitive in all of us.”
                      George Plimpton, in the Paris Review

"Vali's dogs, Vali's trees, Vali's donkey, the birds, the flowers, the caves, the spiders of Vali.  We have seen for the first time the old skeleton of nature."         
                     Bernardo Bertolucci,  film-maker,
                     Last Tango in Paris, Stealing Beauty

For thirty years, Gianni Menichetti, the author of this memoir, lived with Vali Myers in the wild canyon of ‘Il Porto’ – first as a sort of willing slave, then as lover, and finally as friend, confidant, and protector. In this frank, loving and fascinating book, he gives us Vali and the Valley. Gianni is an artist and poet, whose work is represented in numerous private collections in Europe, America and Australia.

CHILDREN'S ART COMPETITION
AND CALENDAR 2005

On October 29th, the Golda Foundation, held the judging for its first children's art exhibition at the Valley Oak Elementary School, located in north Fresno.

The Golda Foundation produced a calendar of the juried children's art work and donated 400 to the Parent's Club to promote further fund raising efforts for the school. The Golda Foundation will offer this project along with other visual and literary art projects to different schools in the community.

GOLDA FOUNDATION'S FIRST ANNUAL PAINTING & POETRY COMPETITIONS, PAINTING EXHIBITION & GALA 2004

The painting exhibition consisted of works by the juried competition winners, first place winner Marilyn E.H. Hochhauser, runner-ups, Joan K. Sharma, Laura Swytak and Jenny Balisle along with works by Nancy Calef, Elizabeth Dante, Paul Graf, Aung Aung Taik, Linda McCoy, Naomi Wright and Diane Abbott.

The competition winners were also displayed on The Golda Foundation's on-line gallery for one year.

Painting & Poetry Competitions Judges:
Jacquelin Pilar, Associate Curator of Fresno Art Museum
Anne Scheid, Drawing Instructor, Fresno City College
Charles Hanzlicek, Author of 8 books of poetry; retired Prof. Cal State University, Fresno
Peter Everwine, Award winning author; retired Prof. Cal State University, Fresno

On October 21, 2004, The Foundation held its first annual gala evening for the painting exhibition and poetry competition at the Fresno Art Museum. Winners were announced, awards were given, and award winning poetry was read by authors, Elizabeth Sosaya and Dixie Salazar. Jody Weiner also read from his new novel, Prisoners of Truth. Live music by Tower Jazz Ensemble in the Sculpture Garden, delicious wines and delicacies from Palma Restaurant.
