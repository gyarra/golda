Edgar Oliver

 



The Man Who Loved Plants

The Man Who Loved Plants is a story where love is pure horror. Set in the hidden world of a botanical garden, the protagonist keeps out of sight while secretly watching the life of the garden's director. It is an obsession leading to a collision of spirits unlike any other.

*** (Excerpt)

The train station was so large as to contain its own weather — a weather of high marble walls that held the same chill in winter and in summer  — the weather of a concrete vault so massive as to have become the sky, beneath whose distance, dim-lit curve a fog seemed to gather.  Above all, it was the weather of echoes — all sounds hollowed and shattered so that words got lost and yet continued sounding long after those who spoke them had taken their trains and gone.  

Above the colonnade that buttressed these marble walls, at the point where the concrete vault launched into the void, giant stone warriors encircled the waiting room.  They stood at intervals, naked, staring sternly ahead.  Each warrior held a shielded in front of his sex.
